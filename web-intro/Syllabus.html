Module 2 - Exploratory Data Science 

Lecture 12: Exploring gene expression in breast cancer

Goal: Develop skills from descriptive statistics to explore biological data. Topics include: visualization, differential expression, clinical outcome, hypothesis testing (t-test, Wilcoxon, Kolmogorov-Smirnoff tests in R).
  • JBStatistics video series on YouTube
  • Learn Statistics video series from Mike Marin (R specific)
  • Z scores (jbstatistics on YouTube)
  • t-distribution (jbstatistics on YouTube)
  • Using R with the t-distribution (jbstatistics on YouTube)
  • One-mean t-test (jbstatistics on YouTube)
Lectures 13,14: Unsupervised analysis: Discovery subtypes in breast cancer Goal: Understand the concept of clustering and apply it to gene expression data. Topics include: hierarchical clustering, k-means clustering, visualization through heatmaps.
Lecture 15: Supervised Analysis: Naive Bayes' classifier Topics include: clinical applications such as predicting patient prognosis or patient benefit from therapy.

Module 3 - Computational Biology

Lecture 16: Next generation sequencing analysis. Goal: Understand (at a high level) a statistical linear model to detect differences in gene expression between two classes with single cell RNA-sequencing data (scRNA-seq). Topics include: RNA-seq, CPM, RPKM, FPKM, TPM
  • "What began as “blue-sky” research in the Chemistry Department of Cambridge University ..."
  • Illumina Sequencing in depth tutorial
  • Sequencing by Synthesis (movie top right)
  • How competition improves DNA sequencing

Links to bioinformatics tools:

  • UCSC Genome Browser
  • UCSC Cancer Browser
  • Integrative Genomics Viewer (IGV)

Links to relevant genomics:

  • EBI course on Next Generation Sequencing
  • EMBO course on NGS Data Analysis
  • Canadian Bioinformatics Workshop of NGS Data Analysis
Lecture 17: Gene prediction and genome annotation. Goal: Understand computational biology approaches to predicting genes in a genome, and learn a commonly used tool. Topics include: Machine learning through Hidden Markov Models, probabilistic model, likelihood, accuracy.
  • GLIMMER
  • Hidden Markov Models (concise Nature review article)
  • HMMs the Movie
  • HMMs in R
Lecture 18: Sequence Alignment Goal: Understand at a high level how pairs of sequences are alignment, and understand how to use common tools in the literature. Topics include: scoring matrices, gaps, dynamic programming, BLAST, BLAST database search, DNA versus amino acid level alignments.
  • BLAST wiki.
  • The BLAST Handbook.
  • BioStars: BLAST.
  • BLAST using R (annotate package)
  • Multiple Sequence Alignment Tools
  • CLUSTAL
  • MUSCLE
Lecture 19: Phylogenetics Goal: Understand the basics of how species phylogenies are inferred computationally, and learn to use a popular tool. Topics include: trees, neighbour joining, outgroups.
  • MEGA
  • Tree of Life Project (also here and here)
Lecture 20: Protein Domain and Structure Prediction (DID NOT COVER in 2018) Goal: Understand the basics of structural and evolutionary approaches to defining subregions of proteins. Topics include: substrings, secondary structure, evolutionarily conserved folds, HMMs.
Lecture 21: Pathway Analysis Goal: Understand the basic approaches to investigating a list of genes for enrichment in biological processes and functions. Topics include: enrichment, depletion, signatures, pathways, gene ontologies.
  • Gene Ontology & AmiGO2
  • GSEA & MSigDB
  • GSVA
  • Global test
  • Hypergeometric test
  • Fisher's Exact Test
  • GSEA/MSigDB
  • Kolmogorov-Smirnoff Test
  • Gene Ontology (GO) with video from EBI
  • Bionomial Distribution with a video providing simple examples
  • Hypergeometric Distribution with a video providing simple examples
  • Chi-squared test with a video
  • Fisher's Exact Test with video
Lecture 22: Deep learning Goal: Discuss (at a high level) deep learning and how it is applied in the biological sciences. Topics include: artificial intelligence, neural networks, deep neural networks, autoencoders.
