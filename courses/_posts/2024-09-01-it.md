__9552: Quantitative Approaches in the Biomedical Sciences (0.5 credits)__

(Enrollment in this course spans both the Fall and Winter terms; students must remain registered throughout the full duration)

Quantitative approaches are now ubiquitous used throughout biomedical research and it has become
increasingly important for biomedical researchers to include training in computation, data science and AI in order to maintain modern research programs. This course is designed to provide
biochemistry graduate students with the opportunity to efficiently learn quantitative tools and techniques
relevant to their research regardless of their prior training background. Topics include programming,
software development, statistics, modelling, visualization, AI, data cleaning, and generative modelling.
